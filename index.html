Seasonal guide to planting in North Carolina

winter

Winter is one of the best times to plant trees and shrubs. The cool moist weather means less watering and more root growth.

Winter planting tip: this is a good time to prune trees, lack of leaves make the brances easier to see and safely cut

Plant Pansies for blooms all winter.(deer love them, so be careful)

spring

Spring is one of the best times to plant. Warm weather and active insects including butterflies make it easy to want flowers!

Spring planting tip: wait until the last frost before planting annual flowers and vegetables

Plant Lantana for blooms all summer. Deer don't eat it.

summer

The sky is the limit during the summer. Fertilize annual plants for the best results and don't forget to water.

Most plants die in summer becuase they aren't getting enough water.

Summer planting tip: Water!

Plant Blackeyed Susans for blooms all summer. They are native too.

fall

Fall is all about leaves. Lots of leaves can cover plants elminating sunlight. This can be harmful if left for an extended amout of time.

Fall planting tip: Trees like leaves. Pile them in beds under trees for free fertilizer.

Plant your spring bulbs like daffodils and tulips

native Plants

Using native plants in your landscape has many benefits. They provide a food source for native animals like birds and insects. They controlled naturally and will not become invasive like kudzu. One of the best things about native plants is the have adapted to this environment and, once established, will need minimal care!

List of plants native to NC

  • Purple Cone Flower
  • Black Eyed-Susan
  • Flowering Dogwood
  • Passion Flower
  • Longleaf Pine

Most native plants do best when planted in spring
